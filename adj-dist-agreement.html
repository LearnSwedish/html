Information

This exercise focuses on predicative adjectives. An adjective becomes predicative when it is not placed directly before the noun it describes. Instead, it appears later in the sentence, introduced by a verb (often together with an adverb). For example:

Subject: The house we saw yesterday
Verb: is / looks /seems
Adverb: rather / quite / brand
Adjective: new.

Remember that in Swedish, most adjectives have three different forms: one for en-gender nouns, a second for ett-gender nouns, and a third for plural (regardless of gender). Because the adjective stands at a distance, you must look back at the subject to ensure they agree in gender/number:
