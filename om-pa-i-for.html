Duration and Temporal Distance

Swedish uses different prepositions depending on whether you express duration (how long something lasts) or temporal distance (how far something is from now) – see this image.

  • om – temporal distance (forward in time)
    Vi ses om två dagar. (We’ll see each other in two days.)
  • på –
    1. duration, often with a sense of quickness or surprise:
      Jag gjorde det på [bara] fem minuter. (I did it in [just] five minutes.)
      → implies it was done faster than expected
    2. temporal distance, always in a negative context:
      Jag har inte gjort det på tre veckor. (I haven’t done it for three weeks.)
      → refers to something that has not happened since a certain time
  • i – duration: time during which something took place
    Jag väntade i 20 minuter. (I waited for 20 minutes.)
  • för ... sedan – temporal distance (backward in time)
    Jag gjorde det för en vecka sedan. (I did it [no preposition] a week ago.)
