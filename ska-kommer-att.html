Information

Swedish has no dedicated future tense; you simply use a present‐tense auxiliary plus the main verb—much as English does with "will [verb]" or "going to [verb]". In practice you choose "ska" for intentions, plans, promises, orders or obligations, and "kommer att" for neutral predictions or statements without personal nuance. Moreover, when the subject is inanimate — books, machines, the weather — you almost invariably use kommer att, since inanimate things lack intention or agency. Time to practise this – in 40 examples.
