Ska eller kommer att?

Read the “Info” to understand when to use ska or kommer att.

"Ska" + [infinitive] or "kommer (att)" + [infinitive]?


FACTS: A large corpus-based analysis shows a clear distribution in Swedish futurity. Around 72% of all future expressions use ska + [infinitive], confirming its role as the default choice for statements involving plans, intentions or decisions. Only about 28% use kommer (att) + [infinitive], which is concentrated in predictions, assumptions and contexts where a less committing tone is preferred. Approximately 15% of all kommer-tokens occur without att, indicating informal usage. The form skall — appearing in only 10% of ska-constructions — is largely confined to formal and legal registers.


Rule 1: When expressing an intention, plan, decision, or obligation, choose ska. BUT NOTE: To sound less committing and more neutral, even when expressing a plan, use kommer att.


Rule 2: When expressing a prediction, guess, or forecast, choose kommer att.


Rule 3: If the subject is inanimate or an animal, use kommer att.



Note

In everyday speech, ska + infinitive and kommer (att) + infinitive often overlap in use.


Vi ska gifta oss i sommar.
Vi kommer att gifta oss i sommar.


Both forms are correct, but the first sounds more factual, while the second sounds more personal and emotionally expressive.
