Swedish Participle Logic

Perfect Participle

The Swedish perfect participle is a grammatical form used to express that an action has been completed, e.g. "Dörren är målad" (The door has been painted) or "En förlorad strid kan inte vändas" (A lost battle cannot be turned around). In English, past participles like "painted" and "lost" serve a similar function, describing completed actions or resulting states. The perfect participle is commonly (but not always) constructed using auxiliary verbs like "är" (is/are) or "blev" (became/was).

In indefinite phrases, the perfect participle takes different forms depending on gender and number. In definite phrases, the same definite form is used, regardless of gender and number. Important to note: vowel changes in certain strong verbs in the perfect tense are also used in the perfect participle.

Example 1: "ar-verbs": måla (paint)
Indefinite Forms Definite Form (same all over)
N gender En måla|d {dörr; vägg; yta} Den måla|de {dörren; väggen; ytan}
T gender Ett måla|t {hus; golv; fönster} Det måla|de {huset; äpplet; huset}
Plural Måla|de {dörrar; hus; fönster} De måla|de {dörrarna; golven; husen}
Example 2: "er-verbs": skriva (write)
Indefinite Forms Definite Form (same all over)
N gender En skriv|en {bok; text; pjäs} Den skriv|na {boken; texten; pjäsen}
T gender Ett skriv|et {brev; avtal; löfte} Det skriv|na {brevet; avtalet; löftet}
Plural Skriv|na {böcker; brev; avtal} De skriv|na {böckerna; breven; avtalen}
Example 3: Strong verbs: försvinna (disappear)
Indefinite Forms Definite Form (same all over)
N gender En försvunn|en {man; kvinna; boll} Den försvunn|a {mannen1; kvinnan; bollen}
T gender Ett försvunn|et {barn; äpple; hus} Det försvunn|a {barnet; äpplet; huset}
Plural Försvunn|a {kvinnor; bollar; hus} De försvunn|a {männen; barnen; träden}

1 In some styles, the perfect participle takes the masculine form when referring to a male person or in a general sense. For example, "den försvunn|e mannen" ("the missing man") or just "den försvunn|e" ("the missing [one]").

Present Participle

The Swedish present participle is a grammatical form used to describe ongoing actions or simultaneous states, e.g., "Han är springande" (He is running) or "Flickan sitter leende" (The girl is sitting smiling). In English, present participles like "running" and "smiling" serve a similar function, describing continuous actions or accompanying states.

In the present participle, we do not see a variety of endings: the form is always the same, regardless of gender and number. However, one must know whether to use the suffix "-a|nde" or "-ende". The first category ("-a|nde") almost entirely dominates, while "-ende" is used for so-called "regular short verbs" (such as sy, bo, må, nå, slå, stå). See the table below for some examples.

Normal verbs Participle suffix -a|nde Short verbs Participle suffix -ende
måla måla|nde bo bo|ende
fråga fråga|nde gå gå|ende
sitta sitta|nde le le|ende
bygga bygga|nde stå stå|ende
köra köra|nde tro tro|ende
