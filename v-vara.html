The verb "vara" (to be)

Read the "Info" to understand what this exercise is about.

Information

This exercise focuses on the different forms of the Swedish verb vara (to be). In Swedish, just like in English, the verb changes depending on the tense (time).

Verb forms in this exercise:

See a full overview here: Eight high-frequency verbs (opens in a new tab)

Split Verb Structures:
Note that the words in compound forms like har varit (has been) or kommer att vara (will be) don't always appear right next to each other. Swedish sentences often place other words between (like the subject or an adverb).

Example: "Hur har din dag varit?"
(How has your day been?)
