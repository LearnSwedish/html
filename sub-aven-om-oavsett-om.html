The Subjunctions “även om” and “oavsett om”

även om and oavsett om both express conditional contrast or indifference — meaning that something happens even if or regardless of whether a certain condition is true.

1. även om — even if

även om introduces a subordinate clause expressing that something happens despite a possible or hypothetical condition. It is very common and corresponds closely to “even if” in English.

  • Jag går ut även om det regnar.
    I’ll go out even if it rains.
  • Vi fortsätter projektet även om pengarna tar slut.
    We’ll continue the project even if the money runs out.
  • Jag skulle stötta dig även om alla andra vände sig bort.
    I’d support you even if everyone else turned away.
  • Hon skulle följa med även om det kostade allt hon äger.
    She’d come along even if it cost her everything she owns.

Register: neutral and frequent; used in both speech and writing.

2. oavsett om — regardless of whether

oavsett om also introduces a subordinate clause, but emphasises indifference: the outcome does not depend on the condition. It often sounds slightly more formal or detached.

  • Jag kommer att resa imorgon oavsett om det regnar eller inte.
    I’ll travel tomorrow regardless of whether it rains or not.
  • Hon tänker delta oavsett om hon blir inbjuden.
    She intends to participate regardless of whether she’s invited.
  • Vi fortsätter arbetet oavsett om någon protesterar.
    We’ll continue the work regardless of whether anyone objects.

Register: more formal; often used in writing or official contexts.

3. Comparison

Both express that an action is independent of a condition, but differ in tone:

  • även om → neutral, everyday use (even if)
  • oavsett om → formal or emphatic, meaning (regardless of whether)

Example comparison:

  • Jag går ut även om det regnar.
    I’ll go out even if it rains.
  • Jag går ut oavsett om det regnar eller inte.
    I’ll go out regardless of whether it rains or not.

4. Summary

  • även om — neutral, common; expresses contrast between condition and action.
  • oavsett om — formal; expresses complete indifference to the condition.

Both correspond to “even if” or “regardless of whether” in English, but oavsett om is used when the speaker wants to stress that the result is unaffected by the condition.
