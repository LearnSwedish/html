The Subjunctions “innan” and “före”

innan and före are both temporal subjunctions that mean “before”. innan is the standard modern form, while före is older and more formal, mainly found in written or historical language.

1. innan — the standard, modern form

innan introduces a subordinate clause describing something that happens before something else. It is the normal, everyday form in both spoken and written Swedish.

  • Jag tog en kopp kaffe innan jag gick till jobbet.
    I had a cup of coffee before I went to work.
  • Hon tog ett djupt andetag innan hon började tala.
    She took a deep breath before she started speaking.
  • Vi lämnade platsen innan någon hann se oss.
    We left the place before anyone could see us.
  • Hon såg sig omkring innan hon klev ut på gatan.
    She looked around before stepping out onto the street.

Register: neutral and modern; used in all styles of Swedish.

2. före — older or formal subjunction

före can also function as a subjunction, meaning “before”, but this use is archaic or stylistically elevated. It appears mainly in formal writing, older literature, or solemn contexts.

  • Före han gick till striden, tog han farväl av sin familj.
    Before he went to battle, he said farewell to his family.
  • Före solen gick upp, hade de redan lämnat byn.
    Before the sun rose, they had already left the village.
  • Hon tänkte länge före hon svarade.
    She thought for a long time before she replied.

Register: formal, literary, or archaic. In contemporary Swedish, this use is largely replaced by innan.

3. Comparison

Both innan and före express that one action precedes another, but they differ in style and frequency:

  • innan → standard, used in all contexts and tenses.
  • före → old-fashioned or formal; found mainly in older or literary texts.

Example comparison:

  • Jag funderar alltid innan jag talar.
    I always think before I speak.
  • Jag funderar alltid före jag talar.
    I always think before I speak. (formal / archaic)

4. Summary

  • innan — modern, neutral subjunction; used in everyday Swedish.
  • före — older or formal subjunction; rare in modern speech.

Both mean “before”, but innan is standard today, while före gives a historical or elevated tone.
