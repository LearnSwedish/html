The Subjunctions “medan” and “samtidigt som”

medan and samtidigt som both introduce time clauses that express simultaneity — that two actions happen at the same time. In English, both correspond to “while”.

1. medan — the common, neutral form

medan is the most frequent subjunction used to express simultaneity. It connects two actions or states happening at the same time, often in contrast or parallel.

  • Karl lagade mat medan jag dukade bordet.
    Karl cooked while I set the table.
  • Hon körde bilen medan han läste kartan.
    She drove the car while he read the map.
  • Han målade staketet medan jag klippte gräset.
    He painted the fence while I cut the grass.
  • Han tvättade fönstren medan jag vattnade blommorna.
    He washed the windows while I watered the flowers.

Register: neutral; used in all styles of Swedish.

2. samtidigt som — emphasising simultaneity

samtidigt som also means “while”, but it gives extra emphasis to the fact that the actions happen at the exact same time. It often sounds slightly more formal or explicit than medan.

  • Han tvättade bilen samtidigt som jag handlade mat.
    He washed the car while I did the shopping.
  • Barnen lekte ute samtidigt som vi lagade middag.
    The children played outside while we cooked dinner.
  • Hon pratade i telefon samtidigt som hon skrev mejl.
    She talked on the phone while writing emails.

Register: slightly more formal or descriptive; often used when the timing of actions is important.

3. Comparison

Both subjunctions express simultaneity, but with a difference in focus:

  • medan → neutral; describes two actions happening at the same time.
  • samtidigt som → emphasises that the actions are exactly concurrent or overlapping.

Example comparison:

  • Han lagade mat medan jag dukade.
    He cooked while I set the table.
  • Han lagade mat samtidigt som jag dukade.
    He cooked at the same time as I set the table.

4. Summary

  • medan — neutral, common; expresses two actions happening at the same time.
  • samtidigt som — emphasises exact simultaneity or parallel actions.

Both correspond to “while” in English, but samtidigt som is slightly more formal or explicit than medan.
