Swedish Future Forms

Intention (Plan) vs. Prediction

We relate to the future in two different ways. When we talk about events we plan to make happen,
Swedish uses ska-tokens, along with a small set of alternative intention verbs. When we talk about
events we predict will happen, Swedish uses kommer-tokens, together with a few other expressions of
likelihood. These two perspectives form the core of how Swedish expresses future meaning.

INTENTION – corresponds to future intention


ska + infinitiv → She is going to + infinitive
Hon ska resa i juni.
She is going to travel in June.
Hon ska köpa en ny telefon.
She is going to buy a new phone.
tänker + infinitiv → She intends to + infinitive
Hon tänker börja träna igen.
She intends to start training again.
Hon tänker sälja bilen.
She intends to sell the car.
avser + infinitiv → She intends to + infinitive (more formal)
Hon avser fortsätta projektet.
She intends to continue the project.
Hon avser lämna in rapporten i tid.
She intends to submit the report on time.
planerar + (att) infinitiv → She is planning to + infinitive
Hon planerar att flytta utomlands.
She is planning to move abroad.
Hon planerar att träffa sina föräldrar.
She is planning to see her parents.
har bestämt sig för att + infinitiv → She has decided to + infinitive
Hon har bestämt sig för att byta jobb.
She has decided to change jobs.
Hon har bestämt sig för att börja studera.
She has decided to start studying.

PREDICTION – corresponds to expectation / likelihood


kommer (att) + infinitiv → She will probably / is likely to + infinitive
Hon kommer att vinna tävlingen.
She will probably win the competition.
Hon kommer att ringa senare.
She is likely to call later.
lär + infinitiv → She will probably / is likely to + infinitive (more formal)
Hon lär komma sent.
She will probably arrive late.
Hon lär klara provet.
She is likely to pass the exam.
ser ut att + infinitiv → She looks as if she’s going to + infinitive
Hon ser ut att somna.
She looks as if she’s going to fall asleep.
Hon ser ut att vinna loppet.
She looks as if she’s going to win the race.

More on tänka


Wait a moment! Doesn’t tänka mean to think?


In Swedish, tänka actually covers two different verbs with two quite separate meanings.


One verb tänka means “to think”, in the sense of reflecting, considering or having something on one’s mind. This is the everyday cognitive verb, corresponding to English think, reflect, consider or ponder.


The other tänka is quite different: it functions as a future-intention verb meaning “to plan” or “to intend”, and it corresponds to English intend to. In this meaning, tänker + infinitive expresses a personal plan or decision and is therefore semantically parallel to ska.


So although the forms look identical, Swedish effectively has two distinct verbs: one meaning to think, and one meaning to intend.
