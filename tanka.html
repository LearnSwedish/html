The Brain-Work Verb: Tänka

A Linguistic Deep-Dive into Sweden's Most Thoughtful Verb

The Essentials

In British English, we often translate tänka simply as "to think", but in Swedish, it acts as a bridge between pure cognition and future intentions.

Form Swedish English Function
Infinitive tänka To think / intend
Present tänker Thinking / plans to
Past tänkte Thought / intended
Supine tänkt Have thought
Imperative tänk! Think! / Imagine!

Three Ways to Use Your Head

1. The Intellectual Mode COGNITION

Directing your mind toward a specific problem or subject. Usually paired with the preposition på (of/about).

"I sat pondering over it."
Jag satt och tänkte på det.

2. The Intentional Mode PLANNING

In Swedish, this replaces "plan to" or "intend to". If you have a plan in your mind, you are thinking it.

"Are you intending to stay all evening?"
Tänker du stanna hela kvällen?

3. The Opinion Mode BELIEF

Used to express a view or a sudden realisation.

"Just as I thought!"
Det var väl det jag tänkte!

Mastering the Particles

A Swedish verb is only as good as its particle. Notice how the meaning shifts:

Tänka efter Reflect, consider, or try to remember. "Think before you act!"
Tänka igenom To think something through thoroughly (genomtänka).
Tänka ut To devise or work out a plan. To "hatch" an idea.
Tänka om To rethink or reconsider a previous decision.

The Reflexive: Tänka sig

When you add sig, you move from reality to imagination. It is used to "picture" or "fancy" something.

"Just fancy that!" / "Imagine that!"
Kan man tänka sig!
