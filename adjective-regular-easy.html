Adjective Agreement (regular): easy version

Read the "Info" to understand what this exercise is about.

Information

In this exercise, you choose the correct form of a Swedish adjective so that it agrees with the noun it describes. You must pay attention to whether the noun is an en-word (common gender) or an ett-word (neuter), and whether the noun is singular or plural, as this affects the adjective ending. The exercise is based on 10 regular adjectives that follow a clear and consistent inflection pattern.


Swedish adjective English meanings
fin (fint, fina)lovely, smart, nice, fine
ful (fult, fula)ugly
trevlig (trevligt, trevliga)pleasant
otrevlig (otrevligt, otrevliga)nasty, rude, unpleasant
dyr (dyrt, dyra)expensive
billig (billigt, billiga)cheap
rolig (roligt, roliga)funny, fun
tråkig (tråkigt, tråkiga)dull, boring, tedious
härlig (härligt, härliga)lovely, delightful, wonderful, glorious
skön (skönt, sköna)comfortable, comfy, pleasant, nice, lovely

Please note!

This exercise includes tooltips with English translations and indicates the gender of each head noun to assist you. For a greater challenge – without these aids – try the harder version of the exercise (see the menu options).
