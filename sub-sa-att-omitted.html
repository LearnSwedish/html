The Subjunction “så att” in Swedish

The Swedish subjunction så att introduces a result or consequence – similar to “so that” or simply “so” in English. In everyday speech, the word att is often dropped.

1. When “så att” is used

In formal or careful Swedish, the full form så att is used to show a result or effect of an action.

  • Hon viskade så att ingen skulle höra.
    She whispered so that no one would hear.
  • Det regnade så kraftigt så att vägen översvämmandes.
    It rained so much so that the road became flooded.

2. When “att” is omitted

In spoken and informal Swedish, att is often left out, especially when the meaning is clear without it. The shortened form så then acts as the link between the two clauses – just like in English.

  • Hon viskade så ingen skulle höra.
    She whispered so no one would hear.
  • Det regnade så kraftigt så vägen översvämmades.
    It rained so heavily that the road became flooded.

3. English and Swedish behave alike

Both languages often drop the second word (att / that) when the meaning remains clear. The difference is mainly one of formality – keeping att sounds a bit more formal or written, while omitting it sounds natural in everyday speech.
