About this exercise

This exercise comprises 60 flip-cards. Each card features a pair of distinct words that "collide" with one another – not least because, in many cases, they are spelled identically. However, there are two key differences. Firstly, the words possess completely different meanings. Secondly – and this is the focus of the exercise – they are pronounced entirely differently, with one employing accent 1 and the other accent 2. For further details on the distinctions between these accents, please click the Accent button in the right-hand menu. On the reverse side of each card you will find additional information about the words, including their respective parts of speech and an example sentence demonstrating their use.

This exercise also serves as a traditional vocabulary drill. You must supply the correct meaning for each word in order to progress to the next card. If you select the correct meaning without having flipped the card, you earn 1 point; otherwise, you receive only half a point for a correct answer.

Good luck!
