What is this?

Swedish (just like for example English, German and Dutch) is an SVO language, which means that its basic sentence structure typically follows the Subject-Verb-Object order (also called straight word order). However, when a sentence begins with an element other than the subject, the verb and subject switch places — this is called inversion. Starting with an adverbial phrase, for example, moves the verb in the main clause before the subject.
