The Subjunctions “när” and “då”

när and då both introduce time clauses and mean “when” in English. när is used for general or repeated situations, while då is used mainly in storytelling for single, specific events in the past.

1. när — general or repeated time

när introduces a subordinate clause that expresses time. It is used for general statements, repeated events, and situations that can occur in the past, present, or future.

  • Det började snöa när jag gick hem från jobbet.
    It began to snow when I was walking home from work.
  • Han blir på dåligt humör när det regnar.
    He gets in a bad mood when it rains.
  • Hon känner sig fri när hon hör fåglarna sjunga.
    She feels free when she hears the birds sing.
  • Jag satt och läste när det knackade på dörren.
    I was reading when there was a knock at the door.

Register: neutral and universal; used in all tenses and contexts.

2. då — narrative, single past events

As a subjunction, då introduces a subordinate clause referring to a single event in the past. It is typical of written or narrative language and marks a specific moment when something happened.

  • Han stannade då han hörde någon ropa.
    He stopped when he heard someone call.
  • Hon log då hon förstod vem det var.
    She smiled when she realised who it was.
  • Det blev tyst då läraren kom in.
    It fell silent when the teacher entered.
  • Alla reste sig då musiken började spela.
    Everyone stood up when the music started playing.

Register: formal or narrative; often used in written storytelling or historical description.
Note: då as a subjunction only refers to the past and usually marks a single, completed event.

3. Comparison

Both express time relations, but they differ in scope and style:

  • när → general, repeated, or ongoing situations (when in general)
  • då → specific, single past events in narratives (when / at the moment that)

Example comparison:

  • När jag var liten, bodde vi i Lund.
    When I was little, we lived in Lund.
  • Vi bodde i Lund då jag var liten.
    We lived in Lund when I was little.

4. Summary

  • när — neutral and versatile; used in all tenses and general contexts.
  • då — restricted to the past; used for single, completed events in narratives.

Both are temporal subjunctions meaning “when”, but när covers general time references, while då focuses on specific, past events typical of storytelling.
