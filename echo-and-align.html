Echo & Align

This exercise focuses on modality and perspective in Swedish. Modality shows how the speaker views an action. For example, "I must go" or "I can go" shows whether an action is a necessity or a possibility.


To provide a correct short answer in Swedish, you must master two things:

1. Echo (The Verb): If a question starts with a modal verb (kan, vill, ska, måste, får, bör) or the verbs vara (to be) and ha (to have), you echo (reuse) that verb in your answer.


Example: "Kan du?" – "Ja, det kan jag."

If the question starts with any other verb, you cannot reuse it. You must use the verb göra (to do).

Example: "Springer du?" – "Ja, det gör jag."

2. Align (The Subject): You must align the subject to the correct perspective. If Person A asks you a question, you answer with "jag" (I). If they ask about a group you are part of, you answer with "vi" (we).

Each card presents a question in Swedish. Your task is to complete the short answer "Ja, det ___ ___." by selecting the correct verb and subject from the dropdown menus. Hover over a card or use the Translation toggle for help.
