Repeat or Adjust

This exercise focuses on modality and perspective in Swedish short answers. Modality indicates how the speaker views an action (e.g. necessity, possibility, or obligation), while perspective refers to the logical shift between the person asking the question and the person providing the answer.


To provide a correct short answer in Swedish, you must master two things:


1. The Verb: Identification and Logic

Swedish short answers follow a logic very similar to English. First, check if the question starts with a special verb (a modal verb like kan, vill, ska, måste, får, bör or the primary verbs vara and ha).


If YES: Just like in English ("Can you?" – "Yes, I can"), you repeat the verb in your answer. If NO: If it is a standard verb (e.g. springer, äter, sover), you must adjust the response by using a form of göra (to do).


2. Selecting the Subject

Imagine that you are always the person being asked. You must adjust the subject to suit the perspective of the conversation:

If the question concerns you (du), you answer with "jag" (I). If the question concerns you and someone else (ni), you answer with "vi" (we), and so on.
