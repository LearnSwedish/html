The Subjunction “att” in Swedish

The Swedish subjunction att corresponds to “that” in English – but it is often omitted, just as in English.

1. When “att” is used

In formal or careful speech and writing, att is often kept to make the sentence clear.

  • Jag tror att hon kommer.
    I think that she’s coming.
  • Han sa att han var trött.
    He said that he was tired.

2. When “att” is omitted

In everyday speech, att is often left out – especially after common verbs such as tro (think), säga (say), veta (know), and hoppas (hope).

  • Jag tror hon kommer.
    I think she’s coming.
  • Han sa han var trött.
    He said he was tired.

3. English and Swedish work the same way

Just like English speakers often drop “that” (“I think she’s coming”), Swedish speakers drop att in similar contexts. It’s a natural feature of informal, fluent Swedish.
