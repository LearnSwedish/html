About this exercise

Among the 10,000 most frequently used Swedish words, there are nearly 800 that feature a rather distinctive stress pattern. In this exercise, these words have been gathered together so you can learn their proper pronunciation.

Some symbols you need to know:

●
indicates an unstressed syllable
●
indicates a weak onset (light stress)
●
indicates the second pitch level in words with Accent 2
⬤
indicates primary stress

Remember (1) that in multi‐syllable words (three or more syllables) with a late primary stress (on the third or a later syllable), the word is often preceded by a weak onset — that is, by the second symbol in the list. Conversely, there is never a weak onset in two‐syllable words. You can find many examples of this among words ending in "-i".

Remember (2) that almost all words beginning with "an-" have Accent 2, which means you must select a pattern that includes the red symbol.

A recommendation: before you go through all the words (or a random 100), start by familiarising yourself with how certain words (those that begin or end in a particular way) are typically pronounced. Of course, there are exceptions, but you'll learn them.

If you hover over the word, you will get its English translation, and you will also see information about its part of speech.
