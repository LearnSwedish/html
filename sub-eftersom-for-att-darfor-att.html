The Subjunction “eftersom” and alternatives

The Swedish causal subjunctions eftersom, för att, and därför att all mean “because”, but they differ slightly in tone, frequency, and nuance.

1. eftersom — the neutral, all-purpose subjunction

eftersom is the most common and neutral way to express cause. It can be used in both spoken and written Swedish, and in all contexts.

  • Vi stannade hemma eftersom det regnade.
    We stayed at home because it was raining.
  • Eftersom det regnade, stannade vi hemma.
    Because it was raining, we stayed at home.

Register: neutral; works well in both formal and informal language.
Note: eftersom is the only one of the three that naturally begins a sentence in Swedish.

2. för att — a causal or purpose subjunction

för att can introduce either a clause of cause (reason) or purpose (intention). The meaning depends on context.

  • Vi stannade hemma för att det regnade.
    We stayed at home because it was raining. (cause)
  • Vi gick hem för att vila.
    We went home in order to rest. (purpose)
  • Hon höll upp paraplyet för att inte bli blöt.
    She held up the umbrella so as not to get wet. (purpose)

Register: slightly more formal than eftersom when used for cause; common and natural when expressing purpose.
Note: unlike eftersom, för att is rarely used at the start of a sentence when it means “because”.

3. därför att — emphatic or spoken

därför att also means “because” but is often used in speech for emphasis or emotional explanation. It can sound slightly less formal than eftersom.

  • Vi stannade hemma därför att det regnade.
    We stayed at home because it was raining.
  • Hon grät därför att hon var trött.
    She cried because she was tired.

Register: conversational; often adds emotional or emphatic tone.
Note: därför att normally appears after the main clause, not at the start of a sentence.

4. Summary

  • eftersom → neutral and most common in all contexts; can start a sentence.
  • för att → can mean “because” (cause) or “in order to” (purpose); rarely starts a sentence when causal.
  • därför att → more emphatic or spoken; follows the main clause.

All three are subjunctions that introduce subordinate clauses, but only eftersom comfortably appears at the beginning of a sentence in standard Swedish.
